Project 2

Supervised Learning - Cervical Cancer Detection Web App: Achieving higher detection reliability with SVM, Border-SMOTE, and Feature Selection

Live Link

Project Overview

Cervical cancer is the fourth most common cancer in women compared to other types of cancer, with an estimated 604 000 new cases and 342 000 deaths in 2020. About 90% of the new cases and deaths worldwide in 2020 occurred in low- and middle-income countries. Born and raised in Vietnam, a developing country counted in that 90%, I wanted to use my knowledge to create something meaningful for my loved women, especially my mom.

The dataset I have from UCI suffers from imbalance with only less than 9% positive patients and approximately 20% missing values. Besides, 32 attributes appear redundant to feed a predictive model, which may lead to potential overfitting. Therefore, several machine learning approaches have been deployed to deal with the aforementioned problems, such as feature engineering, resampling, and feature selection. I found that Support Vector Machine Classification, with the support of Border-SMOTE and Meta-transformer for selecting features based on importance weights for the Hinselmann, shows the most outstanding performance, with 8 chosen features, generating an accuracy of 98.18%.
